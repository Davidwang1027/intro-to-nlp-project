KSC-71PC-543.jpg (234k)
Apollo 15, moments after ignition at 13:34 GMT on July 26, 1971, at launch complex 39A. The launch tower swing arms have not yet retracted, and they continue to hold the Saturn booster in place while the five F-1 engines build up a combined 7,500,000 pounds of thrust.
S71-41356.jpg (125k)
The Saturn rocket rises beside the launch tower with chilly oxygen-fuel vapour streaming along its lower flanks.
KSC-71PC-547.jpg (274k)
Apollo 15 charges into the sky, bound for its target on the moon - the Apennine Mountains and Hadley Rille.

S71-43543.jpg (155k)
This picture freezes the dramatic instant of splashdown at 20:45 GMT on August 7, 1971, and reveals the Command Module pounding a textbook-perfect impact crater into the water of the Pacific Ocean. Liquid crater ejecta flies radially from the impact site. The Command Module landed more heavily than usual owing to the failure of one of its parachutes to open (32 feet per second; normally 28 fps under three inflated canopies).
